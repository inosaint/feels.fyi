# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who want a quick, expressive read on the weather without opening a dense forecast dashboard. They are checking conditions casually on a phone and want the feeling of the weather to land immediately.

## Product Purpose

`feels.fyi` is a hand-drawn illustration based weather app. It communicates the emotion of the weather rather than foregrounding raw data, using playful weather-specific drawings, a large temperature readout, and minimal controls.

Success means a user can open the app and instantly understand what it feels like outside, with the illustration carrying as much meaning as the text.

## Brand Personality

Playful, tactile, expressive.

The app should feel handmade and charming without becoming messy or hard to use. The interface should stay quiet enough that the illustrations remain the emotional center.

## Anti-references

Avoid data-heavy weather dashboards, generic Apple Weather clones, glossy corporate forecast apps, and interfaces that prioritize charts, hourly tables, or metric density over mood.

Avoid decorative polish that fights the hand-drawn art: overly sterile controls, overly complex glass effects, or animation that calls attention to itself.

## Design Principles

- Lead with feeling: the illustration should communicate the weather before the user reads.
- Keep the UI sparse: temperature, condition, and location are enough for the main screen.
- Respect the drawing: typography and controls should support the art, not compete with it.
- Be playful but legible: handmade does not mean imprecise or inaccessible.
- Transition softly: weather arrival should feel like a scene appearing, not a dashboard refreshing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast for all text and controls. Keep touch targets at least 44 points. Support reduced-motion users by avoiding essential information hidden behind animation and by keeping transitions simple and brief.
